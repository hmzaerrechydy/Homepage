Code, eat, sleep, repeat!


Code, eat, sleep, repeat.

Maybe you've seen this statement somewhere on the internet.

It’s seems good and motivates you to spend every minute learning.

For me it’s not!

It’s false for one reason: learning code takes a long time.

That means this method can’t work for the long term, simply because it’s not sustainable there’s other important things we need to take care of like:

  • Exercise
  • Documenting the process: blogging, social media, …etc.
  • Work/Studying
  • Rest

It’s a process that will take years if you want to become a good programmer.

Therefore you should shift your focus from intensity to consistency and balance.

10 to 20 hours a week of learning is great.
